About


Lunar Lander Plus is an Android application that is an extension of the game Lunar Lander by Altari. Application was built as part of a research paper; to determine if social comparision influences in-game performance of players. Results revealed that players with leaderboard accessibility performed with a higher scores than players without.


Source code



What I did:


  • Conducted a series of surveys to gather and collect data on participants to determine external factors that may affect performance.
  • Extended an existing Android implementation of the game Lunar Lander to collect quantitive data.
  • Redesigned the game's basic game mechanics to accomodate for the comparative evaluation between two different interfaces.
  • Updated game's UI for leaderboard options and implemented score systems and levels.

How to Play?

Successfully land the Lunar Lander on the platform. (Sounds easy right?)




The Landing

Successful landing are determined by landing speeds and angles. Not landing perpendicularly will result in a failed landing.




  • Acceptable landing speed is shown as green. Speeds that are in excess are red and will result in a crash.
  • Missing the landing pad will result in a crash. Going offscreen also counts as a crash (currently only horizontally).
  • Landing angles on touchdown that are too severe are registered as a crash.

New levels are available upon successful landings.
Note: See Design (Application) for level details.


Surviving / Game Over

The game is survival-based. Survive as long as you can. You have 5 lives. The game is over when your life total reaches 0.

Controls

  • U     start/pause the game
  • D     exit the game (ignored while playing)
  • L     rotate the lander counterclockwise
  • R     rotate the lander clockwise
  • C     fire the engine (thruster)

Design (Application)

A simple setup page was implemented to gather participant data. Page was purely used by experimentor so its design is very basic.


Scores were recorded based on a multiplier on the amount of time and fuel used to land the spacecraft as follows:

  • Landing score = 100
  • Time score = 5 * (100 / completion time)
  • Fuel score = 5 * (remaining fuel)

To develop a leaderboard, an initial control group was used to build the initial/default leaderboard. Participants who score higher than a score on the leaderboards will replace it; marked by 'P(number)'.


The scoreboard is an option as part of the application’s interface and was available to specific group types along with a setup page.

There are infinite levels. By design, the game is extremely difficult and there were no rewards given out to participants for achieving highest scores. Players are only privy to 5 lives throughout the game. Players cannot earn more lives. Game difficulty increased as the levels progress:

  • Less fuel available at higher level
  • A faster landing is not allowed
  • A greater lander angle is not allowed
  • The landing pad is narrower

The difficulty of levels was designed to engage players via the experiment to determine if players with leaderboard accessibility would play better. An easy game (where similar analytical data is more likely) would make it difficult to compare both control groups.


Data Collection

Participant data is saved and written on the app Astro after the player's 5 lives are used up. Basic implementation for data were calculated and averaged by levels, time, remaining fuel, and scores.
